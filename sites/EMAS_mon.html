The 14th International Workshop on Engineering Multi-Agent Systems (EMAS 2026)

EMAS 2026

May 25–26, 2026
Co-located with AAMAS 2026
Paphos, Cyprus

Scope

EMAS 2026 builds on the long-standing tradition of the Workshop on Engineering Multi-Agent Systems, advancing the design, implementation, and deployment of autonomous agents and Multi-Agent Systems (MAS) through research on theories, architectures, languages, platforms, and methodologies. To build on decades of established MAS engineering approaches and engage with emerging augmented language models and agentic systems, this 14th edition focuses on Hybrid Agent Architectures and Multi-Agent Systems. We invite contributions covering the diversity of approaches to engineering agents and MAS: submissions may focus on established or emerging approaches, as well as hybrid approaches that integrate the two, exploring, among others, questions such as how different agent architectures can be combined to create more capable agents, and how MAS can be designed to ensure interoperability, coordination, and governance among heterogeneous agents.

About the logo
The logo was conceived and designed by Matteo Baldoni, then refined to its final form with the help of generative AI.

The logo represents Aphrodite’s shell: Paphos, the host city of EMAS 2026, is known as the birthplace of Aphrodite, born from a shell. Aphrodite’s shell emerges from a network of agents interacting with each other. Towards the centre of the shell the agents are no longer distinguishable, because they are so strongly connected they behave as a single collective and more capable entity. The colours are teal for the Mediterranean Sea, deep blue for the depths of the sea and of research, and golden yellow for the sandy tones to recall the color of Cyprus in its flag.
